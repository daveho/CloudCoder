Enter the hostname that you will use for your CloudCoder instance.

If you would like to use Duck DNS to provide a DNS hostname for your CloudCoder instance, check the Use Duck DNS checkbox, and then enter your Duck DNS token as shown on your Duck DNS account page. Note that the hostname must a subdomain of duckdns.org, e.g. myserver.duckdns.org. Duck DNS is a free service to provide DNS hostnames for devices whose IP address may change dynamically. Your CloudCoder instance's IP address won't actually change, but Duck DNS is an easy way to get a DNS hostname without registering a domain, configuring a nameserver, etc.

If you do not choose to use Duck DNS, then you will need to configure an A record for the hostname you choose. The wizard will let you know what IP address to use once the webapp server has been provisioned.
